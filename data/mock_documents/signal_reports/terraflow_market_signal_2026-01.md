## Summary
The geothermal market is experiencing renewed growth driven by clean energy mandates and data center demand. TerraFlow operates in an attractive market but faces well-funded competitors with head starts. Timing and differentiation are critical.

## Market Overview

### US Geothermal Market
- **Current Installed Capacity:** ~3.7 GW
- **Addressable Potential:** 500+ GW (DOE estimate)
- **Current Penetration:** <1% of potential
- **Market Size (2025):** $4.2B globally
- **Projected (2030):** $6.8B (6% CAGR)
- **US Share:** ~35% of global market

### Market Drivers

#### Positive Drivers
1. **State/Federal Clean Energy Mandates:** 20+ states with 100% clean energy targets
2. **Data Center Demand:** Hyperscalers seeking 24/7 carbon-free energy
3. **Grid Reliability:** Baseload needs as renewables increase intermittency
4. **DOE Investment:** $74M EGS initiative + IRA incentives
5. **Corporate Sustainability:** Fortune 500 companies committing to clean energy

#### Market Barriers
1. **Long Development Timelines:** 5-10 years for traditional projects
2. **High Upfront Capital:** $4-6M per MW for development
3. **Permitting Complexity:** Multi-agency approvals required
4. **Utility Sales Cycles:** 2-5+ years for PPA negotiations
5. **Technology Risk Perception:** New approaches face skepticism

## Competitive Landscape Deep Dive

### Tier 1: Well-Funded Leaders

#### Fervo Energy
- **Funding:** $280M+ (Series C)
- **Technology:** Horizontal drilling + fiber optic monitoring
- **Status:** First commercial project online (Nevada, 2024)
- **Customers:** Google (115MW announced), utilities
- **Strengths:** Proven commercial operation, strong partnerships
- **Weaknesses:** Higher water requirements, limited to specific geology
- **Threat to TerraFlow:** HIGH - Sets bar for industry credibility

#### Eavor Technologies
- **Funding:** $142M+ (Series B)
- **Technology:** Closed-loop (similar to TerraFlow), but different execution
- **Status:** Pilot operational in Germany; Canada expansion
- **Customers:** European utilities focus
- **Strengths:** International diversification, proven pilot
- **Weaknesses:** Higher costs reported, longer deployment times
- **Threat to TerraFlow:** HIGH - Most directly competitive approach

### Tier 2: Emerging Competitors

#### Quaise Energy
- **Funding:** $63M (Series A)
- **Technology:** MM-wave drilling (similar to TerraFlow), superhot rock targets
- **Status:** Drilling tech development; no integrated system yet
- **Strengths:** Deep expertise in MM-wave drilling
- **Weaknesses:** Very early stage, ambitious depth targets (10-20km)
- **Threat to TerraFlow:** MEDIUM - Similar drilling tech, different application

#### Sage Geosystems
- **Funding:** $30M (Series A)
- **Technology:** Hybrid geothermal + energy storage
- **Status:** Pilot projects in Texas
- **Strengths:** Storage hybrid addresses intermittency
- **Weaknesses:** More complex system
- **Threat to TerraFlow:** LOW - Different approach/market
