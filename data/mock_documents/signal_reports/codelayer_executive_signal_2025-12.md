## VP Engineering Departure Analysis

### Circumstances
- **Announced:** December 2025
- **Destination:** Databricks (Senior Engineering Director)
- **Notice:** 2 weeks
- **Exit Interview:** Described as "opportunity-driven" not "push factors"

### Reported Reasons for Departure
1. Scale of Databricks opportunity (larger team, AI focus)
2. Personal career growth considerations
3. Competitive compensation package

### What We're NOT Hearing
- No signals of conflict with other executives
- No indication of product/strategy disagreements
- No concerns about company trajectory

### Impact Assessment

#### Immediate Impacts (0-90 days)
- **Engineering velocity:** Potential 10-20% slowdown
- **Team morale:** Some uncertainty, but no reported attrition
- **Product roadmap:** On track, but risk of delays
- **Hiring:** Eng hiring may slow without VP advocate

#### Medium-Term Impacts (90-180 days)
- Depends heavily on replacement timing and quality
- If filled quickly with strong hire: minimal lasting impact
- If search drags: could affect Q2/Q3 roadmap

### Search Status
- **Executive search firm:** Engaged (True Search)

**Investment Implication:** Recommend waiting to assess VP Eng replacement quality before new investment. For existing positions, monitor but no immediate action needed.
