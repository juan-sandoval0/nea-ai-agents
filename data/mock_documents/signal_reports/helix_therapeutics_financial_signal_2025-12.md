## Financing Options Assessment

### Option 1: Series D Round
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Timing:** Q1-Q2 2026 (pre-data)
- **Estimated Terms:** $80-120M at $500-700M valuation
- **Pros:** Maintains optionality, buys time past data
- **Cons:** Pre-data dilution; VC market remains selective
- **Investor Appetite:** Mixed signals; existing investors likely to support but may seek discount

### Option 2: Pharma Partnership
- **Likelihood:** Medium-High
- **Timing:** Could close Q1-Q2 2026, or post-data
- **Estimated Terms:** $50-100M upfront + milestones
- **Pros:** Non-dilutive, strategic validation, development support
- **Cons:** May limit future economics, timing uncertainty
- **Status:** Active discussions reported with Pfizer, Roche

### Option 3: Bridge Financing
- **Likelihood:** High (if needed)
- **Timing:** Q1 2026 if gap persists
- **Estimated Terms:** $20-30M, potentially convertible
- **Pros:** Quick execution, maintains optionality
- **Cons:** Typically expensive terms, signals need

### Option 4: Debt Financing
- **Likelihood:** Low-Medium
- **Timing:** Q1 2026
- **Estimated Terms:** $30-50M venture debt
- **Pros:** Non-dilutive, preserves equity
- **Cons:** Covenants, repayment obligations, limited availability for pre-revenue biotech

### Option 5: Royalty/Milestone Financing
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Timing:** Q1-Q2 2026
- **Estimated Terms:** $40-60M
- **Pros:** Non-dilutive, no repayment obligations
- **Cons:** Expensive long-term, complex structuring

## Risk Assessment

### High Risk Scenarios
1. **Phase 2 fails:** Significant value destruction, limited financing options
2. **Partnership delays:** Cash crunch if deal doesn't close in time
3. **Market downturn:** VC financing becomes unavailable

### Mitigating Factors
1. **Strong Phase 1 data:** Supports financing at reasonable terms
2. **FDA Breakthrough:** Regulatory de-risking helps valuation
3. **Pharma interest:** Multiple reported interested parties
4. **Experienced team:** Management has navigated similar situations

## Conclusion
Helix faces a common but manageable biotech financing challenge. The company has multiple viable options, with pharma partnership being the most likely near-term outcome given reported interest. However, any delay in financing creates risk, and investors should model potential dilution scenarios. The Q1-Q2 2026 period will be critical for financing execution.

**Investment Implication:** Any new investment should factor in likely 15-25% dilution before Phase 2 readout. Post-data investment carries different risk profile—higher price if positive, potentially unavailable if negative.
