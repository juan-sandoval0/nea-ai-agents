# TerraFlow Raises $4.5M Seed Round to Advance Next-Gen Geothermal Technology

**Source:** TechCrunch
**Date:** September 22, 2025
**Author:** Tim De Chant

---

TerraFlow Energy has closed a $4.5 million seed round to develop its next-generation geothermal technology, which the company claims can unlock clean baseload power in locations where traditional geothermal isn't viable.

The round was led by Congruent Ventures, with participation from Lowercarbon Capital and the Climate Pledge Fund. The investment comes alongside a $2.1 million grant from the Department of Energy's Enhanced Geothermal Systems program.

"Geothermal is massively underinvested relative to its potential," said Matthew Nordan, partner at Congruent Ventures, in a statement. "TerraFlow's approach could be the key to unlocking geothermal's full promise."

The Denver-based startup, founded in 2024 by former DOE researcher Dr. Maria Santos and ex-Schlumberger engineer Erik Johansson, is developing a closed-loop geothermal system that extracts heat from hot dry rock formations without requiring natural hydrothermal reservoirs.

Traditional geothermal plants rely on naturally occurring underground water and steam, limiting development to specific geological regions—primarily the western United States and countries with volcanic activity. TerraFlow's system uses supercritical CO2 as a working fluid in a sealed loop, potentially enabling geothermal in 90% of US locations.

"The sun doesn't always shine and the wind doesn't always blow, but the earth is always hot," said Santos. "We're making that heat accessible everywhere."

The company has completed lab-scale validation of its technology and plans to use the funding to drill a pilot demonstration well in Nevada in 2026. If successful, TerraFlow expects to pursue a Series A round of $30-50 million to fund commercial-scale deployment.

The geothermal sector has seen renewed investor interest following the success of Fervo Energy, which recently brought its first commercial project online. Other well-funded players include Eavor Technologies and Quaise Energy.

TerraFlow differentiates itself through its millimeter-wave drilling technology—similar to Quaise's approach—combined with what the company claims is a more efficient heat exchange system.

"We're seeing a Cambrian explosion of approaches to next-gen geothermal," said Peter Reinhardt, co-founder of Charm Industrial and a climate tech angel investor not involved in the round. "The market will ultimately decide which technologies win, but the opportunity is massive."

The company has secured letters of intent from three potential customers, including two utilities and a data center operator, though Santos noted these are non-binding expressions of interest dependent on successful pilot results.
