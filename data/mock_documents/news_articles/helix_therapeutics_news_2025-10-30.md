# Helix Therapeutics CFO Warns of "Challenging" Biotech Funding Environment

**Source:** STAT News
**Date:** October 30, 2025
**Author:** Adam Feuerstein

---

Helix Therapeutics CFO Thomas Bennett acknowledged that the biotech funding environment remains "challenging" during a panel at the BIO Investor Forum, even as he expressed confidence in the company's ability to secure additional financing.

"The days of easy biotech money are behind us," Bennett said. "But companies with strong data and clear paths to value creation can still attract capital. We believe we're in that category."

Helix, which is developing mRNA therapeutics for autoimmune diseases, raised $95 million in its Series C round in February 2025 at a $620 million valuation. The company currently has approximately 14 months of runway, though that figure has likely decreased since the panel took place in October.

Bennett's comments come as many biotech companies face difficult financing decisions. Venture capital investment in biotech has declined approximately 30% from peak levels in 2021, and public markets have remained challenging for clinical-stage companies without approved products.

"We're being disciplined about our burn rate while continuing to advance our Phase 2 trial," Bennett said. "Our Phase 1 data and Breakthrough Therapy Designation give us options that not every company has."

The CFO suggested that Helix is exploring multiple financing paths, including additional venture rounds, partnerships with pharmaceutical companies, and non-dilutive funding such as grants and debt.

When asked directly about partnership discussions, Bennett demurred. "We're always having conversations with potential partners. I can't comment on specific discussions, but I'd say there's meaningful interest in our platform."

Industry observers note that Helix's timeline creates some pressure. The company's Phase 2 data readout isn't expected until Q3 2026, meaning any financing decision will need to be made before definitive clinical data is available.

"It's the classic biotech dilemma," said a venture investor at the conference who asked not to be named. "Do you raise now at potentially lower terms, or do you try to stretch to the data and raise from a position of strength? Both options have risks."

Bennett acknowledged the tension but expressed optimism about Helix's position. "We have a differentiated platform, strong data, and a clear regulatory path. That's a combination that attracts capital even in tough markets."
