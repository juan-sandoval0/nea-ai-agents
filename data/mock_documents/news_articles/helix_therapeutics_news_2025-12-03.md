# Pfizer, Roche Said to Express Interest in Helix Therapeutics Partnership

**Source:** Endpoints News
**Date:** December 3, 2025
**Author:** John Carroll

---

Pfizer and Roche have both held preliminary discussions with Helix Therapeutics about potential partnerships for the biotech's mRNA-based autoimmune platform, according to people familiar with the matter.

The discussions, which are described as early stage, reflect Big Pharma's growing interest in next-generation approaches to autoimmune disease. Helix's lead candidate HLX-201 has generated excitement following its Phase 1 results and FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation.

Neither Pfizer nor Roche would comment on the discussions. A Helix spokesperson said the company "regularly engages with potential partners" but declined to comment on specific conversations.

Several sources indicated that any deal would likely wait until after Helix's Phase 2 data readout, expected in Q3 2026. However, one person familiar with Pfizer's thinking suggested the company might consider an earlier deal to secure access to the platform before the data.

"The Phase 1 data is already quite compelling," said a former Pfizer business development executive who spoke on condition of anonymity. "If you wait for Phase 2, you'll pay a much higher price—assuming the data is positive."

Pfizer has been actively rebuilding its autoimmune franchise following setbacks with some of its JAK inhibitor programs. The company's Xeljanz has faced safety concerns that have limited its use, creating an opening for safer alternatives.

Roche, meanwhile, has shown strategic interest in mRNA therapeutics beyond vaccines. The Swiss pharma giant has been exploring applications in oncology and autoimmune disease as it looks to diversify its biologics portfolio.

For Helix, a partnership could address its near-term financing needs. The company has approximately 12 months of runway at current burn rates, and while it could likely raise additional venture capital, a pharma partnership would provide both capital and clinical development resources.

Analysts estimate that a licensing deal for HLX-201 alone could be worth $50-100 million upfront with significant milestones, though a broader platform deal could command considerably higher terms.

Other companies reportedly expressing interest include Bristol Myers Squibb and AbbVie, though these discussions are described as more preliminary.
