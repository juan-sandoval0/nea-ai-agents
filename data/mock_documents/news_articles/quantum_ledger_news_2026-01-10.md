# Quantum Ledger Surpasses $2B in Annual Payment Volume

**Source:** Fintech Times
**Date:** January 10, 2026
**Author:** Rachel Morrison

---

Quantum Ledger, the New York-based cross-border payment platform, announced today that it has processed over $2 billion in total payment volume in 2025, marking a significant milestone for the Series B company.

The achievement represents 140% growth over 2024 volumes and positions Quantum Ledger as one of the fastest-growing B2B payment platforms in the cross-border space.

"Businesses are fed up with the inefficiencies of traditional international payments," said CEO Michael Okonkwo in a statement. "They're losing money to hidden fees, waiting days for settlements, and dealing with a lack of transparency. Our platform solves these problems."

The company's hybrid approach—combining traditional banking rails with blockchain-based settlement—has proven attractive to mid-market businesses looking for faster, cheaper international payments. Average settlement times on the platform are under 4 hours, compared to 3-5 days for traditional wire transfers.

Quantum Ledger also announced the expansion of its trade financing product, QuantumCredit, which launched in Q4 2025. The product allows businesses to access working capital based on their payment history on the platform, with early results showing strong adoption among logistics and manufacturing customers.

"Trade finance is a natural extension of our payment rails," said Okonkwo. "We see the cash flow data in real-time, which allows us to offer competitive financing terms."

The company currently serves 890 businesses across various industries, with a focus on companies with international supply chains. It holds money transmitter licenses in 38 US states and is awaiting EU authorization.

Despite the growth, Quantum Ledger continues to face scrutiny from regulators. The company is currently responding to an inquiry from the New York Department of Financial Services regarding its blockchain settlement practices, though executives have characterized the inquiry as routine.
