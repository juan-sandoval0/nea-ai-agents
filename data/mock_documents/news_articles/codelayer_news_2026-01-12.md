# CodeLayer Lands $1.2M Deal with Major Retailer, Signals Enterprise Momentum

**Source:** TechCrunch
**Date:** January 12, 2026
**Author:** Ron Miller

---

CodeLayer, the Seattle-based developer platform startup, has closed its largest deal to date—a $1.2 million annual contract with a Fortune 100 retailer—signaling growing enterprise appetite for internal developer platforms.

The deal, first reported by TechCrunch, represents a significant milestone for the Series B company, which has been pushing upmarket since its $42 million funding round in March 2025. The retailer, which CodeLayer declined to name, employs over 3,000 developers and selected CodeLayer to standardize its Kubernetes deployment processes.

"This is exactly the type of customer we built CodeLayer for," said CEO Ryan Mitchell. "Large enterprises with thousands of developers struggle with Kubernetes complexity. Our platform gives them the developer experience of a startup while maintaining enterprise governance."

The deal comes at an important time for CodeLayer, which recently saw the departure of VP Engineering Daniel Okafor. Sources say the company has accelerated its search for a replacement and has engaged an executive recruiting firm.

"We have a deep engineering bench and strong technical leadership in Lisa [Park, CTO]," Mitchell said when asked about the departure. "We're being thoughtful about finding the right person to continue scaling our engineering organization."

CodeLayer's platform helps companies build internal developer portals—essentially self-service interfaces that abstract away infrastructure complexity. The approach, sometimes called "platform engineering," has gained significant traction as companies struggle with the operational burden of Kubernetes.

Industry analysts note that the market is heating up, with competitors like Humanitec, Port, and Cortex all raising significant funding. Spotify's open-source Backstage project has also gained enterprise adoption.

"There's a clear market need here," said Jyoti Bansal, CEO of Harness and a veteran of the DevOps space. "The question is which approach wins—pure SaaS like CodeLayer, open-source foundations like Backstage, or some hybrid."

CodeLayer reports ARR of approximately $14 million with 89 paying customers, including several Fortune 500 companies. The company says its net revenue retention exceeds 135%, indicating strong expansion within existing accounts.
