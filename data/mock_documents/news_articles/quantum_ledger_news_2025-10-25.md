# Quantum Ledger Launches Trade Finance Product, Eyes Path to Profitability

**Source:** Forbes
**Date:** October 25, 2025
**Author:** Jeff Kauflin

---

Quantum Ledger is expanding beyond payments with the launch of QuantumCredit, a trade financing product that could accelerate the company's path to profitability.

The new offering provides working capital to businesses based on their payment history on the Quantum Ledger platform, with credit lines ranging from $50,000 to $5 million. The company says it can approve loans in as little as 24 hours, compared to weeks for traditional trade finance.

"We're sitting on incredibly rich data about our customers' cash flows and payment patterns," CEO Michael Okonkwo told Forbes. "That allows us to underwrite risk in ways traditional lenders can't."

The move into lending represents a significant strategic shift for Quantum Ledger, which has focused exclusively on payments since its founding in 2021. Trade finance is a massive market—estimated at $10 trillion globally—that remains underserved for small and mid-sized businesses.

CFO Robert Kim said the company has allocated $25 million for its initial loan book, with plans to expand based on performance. "We're being conservative and starting with our highest-quality customers," Kim said. "Our loss rate assumptions are in the low single digits based on the data we're seeing."

The trade finance product carries significantly higher margins than the company's core payments business. While Quantum Ledger's payments gross margin sits around 62%, early projections suggest QuantumCredit could achieve gross margins above 80%.

"Fintech companies that successfully add lending to payments typically see dramatic improvements in unit economics," said an analyst at Autonomous Research. "Square, Stripe, and others have shown this playbook works."

The launch comes as Quantum Ledger's core payments business continues to grow, with the company on track to process over $2 billion in payment volume this year. The company raised $52 million in its Series B round in June, led by Ribbit Capital.

However, the expansion into lending also introduces new risks, including credit risk and additional regulatory requirements. The company has hired several risk management executives in recent months to build out its lending infrastructure.
