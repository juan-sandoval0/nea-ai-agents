# CodeLayer VP Engineering Departs for Databricks Amid Scaling Push

**Source:** The Information
**Date:** December 10, 2025
**Author:** Amir Efrati

---

CodeLayer's VP of Engineering Daniel Okafor has left the developer platform startup to join Databricks as a senior engineering director, according to people familiar with the matter.

The departure comes at a critical time for CodeLayer, which raised $42 million in Series B funding earlier this year and has been aggressively scaling its engineering organization. Okafor, who joined CodeLayer in 2022 and helped grow the engineering team from 8 to 45 people, was considered a key leader in the company's technical organization.

CodeLayer CEO Ryan Mitchell confirmed the departure in a statement to The Information: "Daniel made significant contributions to CodeLayer and we wish him well at Databricks. We have a strong engineering leadership team and are conducting a thorough search for his replacement."

People familiar with Okafor's decision said he was attracted by Databricks' scale and the opportunity to work on AI infrastructure. Databricks has been on an aggressive hiring spree as it expands its data and AI platform.

The timing of the departure has raised questions among investors and industry observers. CodeLayer has been in the midst of a critical scaling phase, with significant enterprise customers coming online and product expansion underway.

"Losing your VP Engineering at this stage is never ideal," said one venture capitalist familiar with the company who asked not to be named. "The key is how quickly they can find a replacement and whether the team can maintain velocity in the meantime."

CodeLayer CTO and co-founder Dr. Lisa Park has assumed interim responsibility for the engineering organization. Sources say the company is working with an executive search firm and hopes to fill the role within 90 days.

The departure follows an otherwise strong year for CodeLayer. The company has grown ARR to over $14 million, secured several Fortune 500 customers, and achieved SOC 2 Type II certification. Last month, it launched AI-powered features for automated deployment recommendations.

CodeLayer competes with other developer platform startups including Humanitec, Port, and Cortex, as well as open-source alternatives like Spotify's Backstage.
