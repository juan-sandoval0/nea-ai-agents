# AWS Announces AI Gateway Preview, Sparking Competition Concerns for Startups

**Source:** The Information
**Date:** December 8, 2025
**Author:** Kate Clark

---

Amazon Web Services unveiled a preview of its AI Gateway service at re:Invent this week, a move that sent ripples through the ecosystem of startups building similar infrastructure for managing large language model deployments.

The new AWS service offers unified API access to multiple LLM providers, intelligent routing, and cost management—features that directly overlap with offerings from venture-backed startups like Nexus AI, Portkey, and Helicone.

"When AWS enters your market, it's always a signal to pay attention," said a partner at a prominent Silicon Valley venture firm who asked not to be named. "But we've seen this movie before. Startups with strong product velocity often maintain their edge."

Nexus AI CEO Dr. Sarah Chen addressed the competitive development in a company all-hands meeting, according to a person familiar with the matter. Chen reportedly emphasized the company's 18-month head start, deep integrations with enterprise customers, and advanced features not yet available in the AWS preview.

"AWS is validating the market, which is actually helpful for us in enterprise sales conversations," Chen said on the company's LinkedIn page. "We're focused on continuing to ship faster and stay ahead on features that matter to developers."

The startup, which raised $14 million from Andreessen Horowitz in October, has been growing rapidly, with sources indicating ARR approaching $3 million. However, the AWS announcement introduces new uncertainty into the competitive landscape.

Not all investors are concerned. "The LLM infrastructure market is massive and growing," said an investor who asked not to be named. "There's room for multiple winners, and often specialized players serve developers better than platform incumbents."

AWS's AI Gateway is expected to reach general availability in mid-2026.
