# Helix Therapeutics Reports Strong Phase 2 Enrollment, On Track for 2026 Readout

**Source:** BioPharma Dive
**Date:** January 8, 2026
**Author:** Sarah Owens

---

Helix Therapeutics announced that its Phase 2 trial of HLX-201 for rheumatoid arthritis has enrolled 180 of 240 planned patients, putting the study on track for a data readout in Q3 2026.

The Cambridge-based biotech said enrollment is proceeding faster than initially projected, with strong interest from both investigators and patients. The company attributed the momentum to the promising Phase 1 results and unmet need in the rheumatoid arthritis space.

"We're seeing tremendous enthusiasm from the clinical community," said Dr. Katherine Reyes, Chief Medical Officer. "Patients and physicians are eager for new treatment options, particularly ones that could offer disease modification without the safety concerns of current therapies."

HLX-201 received FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation in March 2025, based on Phase 1 data showing that 68% of patients experienced significant reduction in disease activity at 24 weeks. The therapy uses a novel mRNA approach to induce immune tolerance to disease-specific antigens.

The Phase 2 trial is evaluating two doses of HLX-201 versus placebo in patients with moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis who have had inadequate response to conventional treatments. The primary endpoint is ACR50 response at 24 weeks.

Dr. Anthony Fauci, a rheumatology expert at Johns Hopkins not involved in the trial, called the approach "scientifically compelling." He noted, "If Helix can demonstrate disease modification in Phase 2, it would be a significant advance for patients who currently have limited options."

The company currently has approximately $72 million in cash, which it expects will fund operations into early 2027. Analysts note that Helix may need to raise additional capital or secure a partnership before the Phase 2 readout.

"The Phase 2 data will be critical for Helix's financing options," said a biotech analyst at Jefferies. "Positive results could attract significant partnership interest or support a strong Series D round."

Helix is also advancing its pipeline, with an IND filing for its multiple sclerosis program expected in Q4 2026.
