# TerraFlow Energy Secures Nevada Pilot Site, Permitting Approved

**Source:** Canary Media
**Date:** December 18, 2025
**Author:** Jeff St. John

---

TerraFlow Energy, a Denver-based geothermal startup, has secured all necessary permits for its pilot demonstration project in northern Nevada, clearing a key hurdle ahead of drilling operations planned for next year.

The Bureau of Land Management approved the company's pilot site in Pershing County, Nevada, following an environmental assessment that found the project would have minimal impact on surrounding lands. The approval allows TerraFlow to proceed with drilling its first demonstration well in Q2 2026.

"This is a significant milestone for our company and for next-generation geothermal," said CEO Dr. Maria Santos. "Permitting can often be a multi-year process, but our closed-loop system's minimal environmental footprint helped accelerate approval."

TerraFlow's technology uses a closed-loop system that circulates working fluid through deep wells in hot dry rock, extracting heat without requiring natural hydrothermal reservoirs or water injection. The approach could theoretically enable geothermal power generation across most of the continental United States.

The pilot project is partially funded by a $2.1 million grant from the Department of Energy's Enhanced Geothermal Systems program, announced in September. The company raised $4.5 million in seed funding the same month from Congruent Ventures, Lowercarbon Capital, and the Climate Pledge Fund.

"Geothermal is the sleeping giant of clean energy," said Matthew Nordan, partner at Congruent Ventures. "TerraFlow's technology could unlock baseload renewable power for regions that currently have no geothermal options."

The Nevada pilot will drill to approximately 3,000 meters depth, targeting temperatures above 200°C. If successful, the demonstration could generate up to 5 MW of power—enough to power approximately 4,000 homes.

TerraFlow faces competition from better-funded startups like Fervo Energy, which recently brought its first commercial project online in Nevada, and Eavor Technologies, which is developing a similar closed-loop approach. However, TerraFlow claims its drilling technology and heat exchange system offer superior economics.

"We're not trying to win by being first," Santos said. "We're focused on being the lowest-cost solution. That's what will matter for scaled deployment."

The company plans to seek Series A funding following the pilot demonstration, with a target of $30-50 million to fund commercial deployment.
