IMPORTANCE VS. URGENCY WEIGHTING:
* Weight importance at roughly 60% and urgency at roughly 40%.
* Both importance and urgency factor into the priority score, but importance carries more weight than urgency.
* A highly important but non urgent email (e.g., a strategic LP relationship opportunity, a compelling deal from a top tier founder) should score higher than a moderately important but urgent email (e.g., a routine scheduling request with a deadline).
* An email can be P75 or even P90 on importance alone if the stakes are high enough, even if there is no immediate time pressure.
* "Urgent" in the email text is NOT evidence of urgency. Infer urgency from OBJECTIVE signals only (explicit deadlines with dates, meetings scheduled within 24 hours, transaction timing, IC votes).

THE FOUR PRIORITY TIERS:
* 90 to 100 (P90): RESERVED FOR TRULY URGENT, high stakes items requiring critical action. The bar for P90 is very high; most emails belong in P75 or below. Includes interesting deal flow that merits a look, routine professional correspondence from known contacts, follow ups, and anything that is useful but not urgent. The bar here is still meaningful; if this investor would not notice or care if he never replied, it is P0.
* 0 to 54 (P0): FYI ONLY, NO REPLY NEEDED. Includes mass emails, newsletters, automated notifications, generic outreach from unknown senders, low signal cold pitches, and anything this investor would not miss.
